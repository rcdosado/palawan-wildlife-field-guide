Palawan Heritage

Palawan is considered as the last ecological frontier of the Philippines due to its undeniably rich natural resources. The province is composed of more or less 1,700 islands which measures approximately 1.5 million hectares. Almost 46% of the total land cover of the province are forest areas- a large portion of which are classified as indigenous vegetation, rainforest, mangroves, and coastal ecosystem (PCSD, 2016)

What is the Project About

The project aims to raise environmental awareness and promote wildlife conservation actions in the province through publication and dissemination of key information about high-value wildlife. This information will also help managers and decision makers to develop appropriate activities such as crafting of policies and management plans and strategies leaning towards habitat protection and biodiversity conservation

View details »

How to do it

In order to expedite the management and utilization of wildlife data and information, the project intends to establish a centralized database of studies/researches on identified Palawan wildlife. The database will be most useful in storing and managing the updated information about Palawan wildlife
